## What SignCode Pro Is
A **sign permit research and job tracking platform** built specifically for Florida sign companies.

**Core value prop:** You type in an address or city, it identifies the jurisdiction, and instantly surfaces:
- Sign code requirements (max heights, setbacks, sq ft limits, EMC/digital rules)
- Required documents (exact checklist for that sign type + jurisdiction)
- Red flag alerts (Master Sign Programs, overlay districts, engineer seals, adjacent municipality approvals)
- Direct contact info (right department, right number, right portal)
- Fees and turnaround times
- Practitioner notes (real-world gotchas from the field)

**The pain it solves:** Sign permit coordinators waste 2–4 hours per unfamiliar jurisdiction digging through municipal websites. Rejected submittals add weeks to jobs. Installers show up and can't work because the permit wasn't right.

## The Lookup System

### How it works:
1. User types a city (e.g. "Hollywood")
2. CITY_MAP in lookup/page.tsx maps it to a jurisdiction key (e.g. "hollywood")
3. Calls /api/lookup?jurisdiction=hollywood
4. API route has hardcoded verified data for each jurisdiction
5. Sends that data to Claude (claude-sonnet-4-6) to format cleanly
6. Falls back to raw data if AI call fails
7. fetchSource() tries to pull from official government URLs for supplemental context

### 18 jurisdictions currently covered:

**Original 10:**
- miami-dade — Miami-Dade County (engineer seal over 24 sq ft, adjacent municipality approval, shop inspection)
- broward — Broward County (unincorporated only)
- palm-beach — Palm Beach County (tiered system by road classification)
- fort-lauderdale — City of Fort Lauderdale (contractor registration required, interior signs need engineer seal)
- pompano-beach — City of Pompano Beach (Master Sign Program mandatory since 2022, AAC review for Atlantic Blvd)
- boca-raton — City of Boca Raton (strictest in South FL, EMC banned, design review, 8-12 week turnaround)
- miami-beach — City of Miami Beach (no EMC/digital, historic districts, channel letters only)
- orlando — City of Orlando (Downtown CRA requires Appearance Review Board)
- tampa — City of Tampa (historic districts need ARC Certificate of Appropriateness)
- hillsborough — Hillsborough County (always verify unincorporated vs Tampa city limits)
